MEDICAL ROBOTS

A robot is a machine resembling a human being and able to replicate certain human movements and functions automatically

Therefore a medical robot is a robot used in the medical sciences. These types of robot mainly perform an assisting role and as an extra tool for the surgeon

Medcial robots allow the surgeons to have an increased precision, which makes complex surgeries easier, or possible, to perform. Another advantage is that the recovery is faster as there is less blood loss, less risk of infection and less post-surgery pain.

The first surgical robot was "PUMA 560" used in 1985. At the moment, the most commonly used surgical robot is the "Da Vinci" surgical system.

3D PRINTED ORGANS

3D printing is, as we all know, the action or process of maming physical objects from a three-dimensional digital model, typically by laying down many thin layers of a material in sucession.

However, organ 3D printers are new innovative printers currently in development that consists on using bio-ink and bio materials yo create functioning organs.

Bio-inks are materials used to produce engineered and artificial live tissue that is used in 3D printing. These inks are mostly composed of cells and other biomaterials that mimic the ectracellular matriz environment.

PROSTHETICS

Prostheses are artificial body parts, designed to restore the function or appereance. When talking about artificial limbs, there are usually 4 main types to consider: transradial, transfemoral, transtibial and transhumeral.

Many companies use computer-aided design and manufacturing to fabricate prosthetics. Also, there are many technologies used in the creation of these, for example:

  • robotics
  • 3D printing
  • artificial inteligence
  • virtual reality
  • motion sensing
